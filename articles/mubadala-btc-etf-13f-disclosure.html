Mubadala Investment Company Files 13F Disclosing $565M BlackRock Bitcoin ETF Position

REGULATORY DISCLOSURE · 3 MIN READ · APRIL 15, 2025
SHARE

Mubadala Investment Company, the Abu Dhabi sovereign wealth fund managing approximately $302 billion in assets, filed its Q4 2024 Form 13F with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission on February 14, 2025, disclosing a position of 8,726,972 shares in the iShares Bitcoin Trust ETF (IBIT) valued at $565.7 million as of December 31, 2024.

Filing Details and Position Characteristics

The 13F filing, submitted to the SEC via EDGAR on February 14, 2025 (accession number 0001720716-25-000008), lists the IBIT position as a sole voting and investment discretion holding. The position represents a new entry in Mubadala's U.S. equity disclosures — the fund did not hold IBIT shares in its Q3 2024 13F filing, indicating the position was established during Q4 2024, a period when IBIT saw significant institutional inflows following the U.S. spot Bitcoin ETF approval in January 2024.

At the December 31, 2024 disclosure date, IBIT shares were priced at approximately $64.80. The 8,726,972-share position implies an average acquisition cost below that level, given Bitcoin's price trajectory during Q4 2024 which saw Bitcoin reach $107,000 in December before the filing cutoff date.

Sovereign Wealth Fund Bitcoin ETF Adoption Context

Mubadala joins a growing list of sovereign wealth funds disclosing Bitcoin ETF positions through U.S. regulatory filings. Norway's Government Pension Fund Global, the world's largest sovereign wealth fund, disclosed indirect Bitcoin ETF exposure through its equity holdings in companies with significant BTC treasury positions. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund has not disclosed direct crypto ETF exposure in U.S. filings as of this date.

The $565.7 million position represents approximately 0.19% of Mubadala's total assets under management — a meaningful initial allocation for an institution of this size, consistent with a position-building approach rather than a full strategic allocation. Market participants have noted that sovereign wealth funds typically establish initial positions below 0.5% of AUM before determining whether to scale exposure based on performance and risk review cycles.

iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) Market Position

IBIT managed by BlackRock became the world's largest Bitcoin ETF by assets under management in 2024, reaching $50 billion in AUM within its first year — a pace unprecedented in ETF history. As of the filing date, IBIT held approximately 572,000 BTC in custody with Coinbase Custody Trust Company as the custodian. The fund charges a 0.25% annual management fee with a temporary sponsor fee waiver for new investors.

Mubadala's 8.73 million shares represent approximately 1.52% of IBIT's total outstanding shares as of December 31, 2024, making it a top-10 institutional shareholder of the fund based on publicly available 13F data aggregation.
