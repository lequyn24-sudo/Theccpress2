DeFi Development Corp Q1 2025 Shareholder Update: SOL Per Share Up 108% YoY

COMPANY RELEASE · 3 MIN READ · APRIL 15, 2025
SHARE

DeFi Development Corp (Nasdaq: DFDV) today released its Q1 2025 shareholder update, reporting that SOL per fully diluted share increased 108% year-over-year, driven by continued accumulation of Solana through staking rewards, open market purchases, and the company's convertible note program. The company now holds 609,993 SOL on its balance sheet as of March 31, 2025.

Treasury Strategy and Accumulation Method

DeFi Development Corp employs a multi-channel SOL accumulation strategy. Staking rewards generated approximately 18,400 SOL during Q1, representing a 7.2% annualized yield on the company's staked position. Open market purchases added a further 43,200 SOL over the quarter, funded primarily through proceeds from the company's at-the-market equity offering. The company's outstanding convertible notes, if fully converted at current terms, would add an additional 82,000 SOL-equivalent exposure to the balance sheet.

The company's SOL per fully diluted share metric — the primary KPI management uses to evaluate treasury performance — increased from 0.41 SOL per share in Q1 2024 to 0.85 SOL per share in Q1 2025. Management has stated that growing this metric, rather than maximizing nominal SOL holdings, is the board-level objective for capital allocation decisions.

Operational Update

Beyond treasury management, DeFi Development Corp completed the integration of its validator node infrastructure in Q1, now operating three active validators on the Solana network. The company's validators processed an average of 2.1 million transactions per day during March, generating commission revenue that supplements staking rewards. Management expects validator operations to reach cash flow breakeven by Q3 2025.

The company also announced a partnership with a Solana-based DeFi protocol to provide liquidity for its treasury SOL holdings, enabling yield generation on assets not currently staked. Details of the partnership will be disclosed in the Q2 filing.

Q2 2025 Outlook

Management guided for continued SOL accumulation in Q2, targeting a SOL per share increase of 15–20% quarter-over-quarter subject to market conditions. The company has authorized a $50 million increase to its open market purchase program. The board has approved no dividend or buyback program for common shares; all capital allocation is directed to SOL accumulation consistent with the company's stated treasury mandate.

The full Q1 2025 shareholder letter and financial schedules are available on the Investor Relations section of the company's website and have been filed with the SEC on Form 8-K.
