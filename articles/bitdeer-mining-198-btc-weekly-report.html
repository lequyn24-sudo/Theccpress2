Bitdeer Mined and Immediately Sold 198 BTC This Week — What the Treasury Decision Reveals

BY EDITORIAL DESK · 7 MIN READ · APRIL 15, 2025
SHARE

Bitdeer Technologies Group's latest weekly mining report disclosed that the company mined 198 BTC and sold 100% of it during the reporting period — a treasury strategy that stands in stark contrast to competitors like Marathon Digital Holdings and CleanSpark, which hold all or most of their mined Bitcoin. The decision to sell immediately is not random; it is a deliberate response to the company's operational cost structure, debt obligations, and hardware expansion plans. Understanding it requires going beyond the headline number.

Why Bitdeer Sells Everything — The Operational Reality

Mining a Bitcoin and selling it immediately is not a sign of weakness — it can be a sign of strategic clarity. Bitdeer operates on a fundamentally different cost structure than its peers. The company is simultaneously expanding its hardware manufacturing division (SEALMINER ASICs), running large-scale data center development, and managing energy contracts that require dollar-denominated payments. Cash flow from mining operations needs to be in dollars, not Bitcoin, to service these commitments without introducing additional currency risk.

The company's Q4 2024 annual report showed total debt of approximately $340 million, with $89 million in current maturities. At a BTC price of $87,400, 198 BTC sold weekly generates roughly $17.3 million in gross revenue. After power costs, staff, and debt service, the operational margin is thin enough that holding Bitcoin on the balance sheet introduces a cash flow risk the company cannot currently absorb. This is the opposite of the MicroStrategy model, which deliberately leverages Bitcoin appreciation to fund its treasury strategy. Bitdeer's primary business is operational, not financial.

The Hold vs. Sell Divide Among Public Miners

The public Bitcoin mining sector is bifurcating into two camps with fundamentally different business models. The first camp — Marathon Digital, CleanSpark, MARA Holdings — operates with large treasury holdings of mined Bitcoin. Marathon disclosed holding over 17,000 BTC on its balance sheet as of Q1 2025, a position worth approximately $1.5 billion at current prices. The thesis is that mining costs provide a below-market Bitcoin acquisition cost, and holding the asset allows the company to benefit from long-term price appreciation without paying a market premium.

The second camp — Bitdeer, Riot Platforms to a lesser degree, Core Scientific post-bankruptcy — operates mining as a cash-flow business. Revenue is denominated in BTC but expenses are in dollars. Selling mined Bitcoin immediately converts operational output into working capital. The risk profile of this model is lower: the company is not exposed to a Bitcoin price decline destroying balance sheet value. The upside is also lower: the company cannot benefit from Bitcoin appreciation on accumulated holdings. Which model is superior depends entirely on where Bitcoin's price goes, which neither camp can predict.

Is Immediate Selling a Stress Signal? The History Says: Sometimes

In previous mining cycles, a pattern of immediate full-liquidation of mined Bitcoin has sometimes preceded financial distress. Core Scientific filed for Chapter 11 in December 2022 after months of selling all mined Bitcoin to cover operational costs — and then running out of runway when prices fell faster than expected. Compute North and Argo Blockchain filed for protection around the same period, with similar patterns in their final months of operation. The pattern is not causal — mining companies sell Bitcoin for many reasons — but it is a monitoring signal worth tracking.

In Bitdeer's case, the immediate concern is manageable but not trivial. The company has publicly communicated its hardware manufacturing ambitions, which require significant capital. If the SEALMINER ASIC business does not generate sufficient external revenue to offset development costs, and if Bitcoin price declines enough to compress mining margins, the company may face a liquidity decision between its mining operation and its manufacturing expansion. The weekly sales report is a data point in a longer thesis, not a crisis signal on its own. But analysts covering the sector are watching the trend carefully.
