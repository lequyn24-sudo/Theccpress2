US Core PPI Rises 5.2% YoY — Bitcoin Drops 4% in 90 Minutes as Macro Fear Spreads

BY EDITORIAL DESK · 6 MIN READ · APRIL 15, 2025
SHARE

The April inflation print came in hotter than anyone on Wall Street had modeled. US core PPI rising 5.2% year-over-year against a consensus of 4.3% was not just a miss — it was a signal that the Federal Reserve's rate-cut timeline could shift materially. Bitcoin, which had climbed to $87,400 in the hours before the data dropped, shed over $3,500 in the 90 minutes that followed.

The Inflation Print That Broke the Rate-Cut Narrative

At 8:30 AM Eastern, the Bureau of Labor Statistics released producer price index data that immediately invalidated two months of market positioning. Core PPI at 5.2% annualized — a measure that excludes volatile food and energy inputs — told traders that inflationary pressure was not simply a supply-chain artifact. It was structural, and it was re-accelerating.

The Fed funds futures market repriced within minutes. Rate-cut probability for the June meeting fell from 38% to 11% on CME FedWatch. Longer-duration assets took the sharpest pain: tech equities dropped 2.1%, gold declined 1.4%, and Bitcoin — increasingly correlated to the NASDAQ risk-on basket — led digital assets into a synchronized sell-off. The 90-minute price action was violent enough to trigger stop-loss cascades across leveraged positions on Binance, Bybit, and OKX.

Why Bitcoin Moved Faster Than Equities

The speed of Bitcoin's reaction relative to equity markets is worth examining carefully. Bitcoin trades 24 hours a day, seven days a week, with no circuit breakers and no market-maker obligations. When macro data drops, the crypto market absorbs the signal without buffers — and in this case, levered long positions had accumulated to a degree not seen since February. Open interest on BTC perpetual swaps had reached $18.4 billion across major venues in the 72 hours before the PPI release.

On-chain data from Glassnode showed that exchange inflows spiked by 31% in the hour following the print, suggesting a combination of panic selling by retail holders and deliberate position reduction by larger accounts. The funding rate on BTC-PERP flipped negative for the first time in 11 days, indicating that short sellers had momentarily seized control of the derivatives market. By 10:45 AM, the sell pressure had largely exhausted itself, and Bitcoin began a slow recovery that would reclaim half the drawdown by close of New York equity trading.

What This Means for the Rest of the Cycle

The more important story is not the 90-minute reaction. It is what the April PPI print does to the macro backdrop that has supported the 2025 bull cycle. Since October 2024, crypto markets had been pricing in a soft-landing scenario: inflation cooling, employment stable, the Fed gradually easing. That thesis is now under serious pressure. Three consecutive months of above-consensus core PPI readings would almost certainly push the first rate cut into late 2025 or early 2026.

Analysts at JPMorgan's digital assets desk noted that Bitcoin has historically performed well even in higher-rate environments, provided that rates are stable. It is the uncertainty — the repricing of the rate path itself — that creates volatility. Until the May CPI and PPI data confirm a trend in either direction, the market will remain in a reactive posture, vulnerable to further macro-driven dislocations. The April print was a reminder that the macro floor beneath this cycle is not as solid as the February rally suggested.
