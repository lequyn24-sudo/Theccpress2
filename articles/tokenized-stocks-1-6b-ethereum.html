Tokenized Stocks Cross $1.6B Market Cap — Ethereum Quietly Captures 41.1% of the New Asset Class

BY EDITORIAL DESK · 8 MIN READ · APRIL 13, 2025
SHARE

The tokenized equities market crossed $1.6 billion in total value locked with little ceremony in early April. Unlike the noisy milestones that punctuate crypto cycles, this one arrived quietly — buried in a Dune Analytics dashboard update that a handful of DeFi researchers noticed before the mainstream financial press. Ethereum's 41.1% market share in this emerging asset class is the number that matters most, and it arrived without Ethereum making a single headline in the past two weeks.

KEY FINDINGS — EVIDENCE LEVEL: MULTI-SOURCE
$1.6B Total tokenized stock market cap as of early April 2025
41.1% Ethereum's share of tokenized equity value
$658M Estimated value of tokenized equities settled on Ethereum
7 Major issuers now offering tokenized US equities across multiple chains

The Milestone Nobody Announced

The $1.6 billion threshold for tokenized equity market cap was not the subject of a press release. No single company controls the metric — it is an aggregate of on-chain activity across multiple issuers including Backed Finance, Ondo Finance, Securitize, and several newer entrants. The figure was first compiled by RWA.xyz, a real-world asset data aggregator, whose dashboard update on April 7 quietly ticked past the milestone. Market researchers at Binance Research picked it up two days later in a sector note that received modest distribution.

The silence is itself meaningful. Tokenized stocks are not a product that any single actor can claim. They represent a category that is being built simultaneously by TradFi infrastructure firms, crypto-native protocols, and registered transfer agents — all operating under different regulatory frameworks. The result is a fragmented but growing market that lacks a single champion to drive its narrative. That may actually be a feature. Markets built without a single promotional voice tend to be more durable.

Why Ethereum Captured 41% Without Trying

Ethereum's 41.1% share of tokenized equity value did not come from aggressive product marketing. It came from Ethereum's existing position as the settlement layer of choice for institutional DeFi activity. The smart contract infrastructure that houses USDC, USDT, and billions in institutional lending protocols was already trusted by the counterparties that matter. When Securitize, BlackRock's tokenization partner, chose where to issue BUIDL — its tokenized money market fund — it chose Ethereum. When JPMorgan chose a public blockchain for its tokenized fund experiments, it chose Ethereum. These decisions compounded.

The remaining 58.9% is divided primarily between Stellar, Polygon, and emerging competitors including Solana and Arbitrum. Stellar holds a disproportionate share due to its early partnerships with Franklin Templeton's BENJI fund. Polygon benefits from regulatory comfort among European issuers who prefer its lower gas environment for smaller transactions. But neither competitor has demonstrated the ability to attract the top tier of institutional issuers that have anchored their programs on Ethereum mainnet.

The Regulatory Vacuum Underneath the Numbers

The $1.6 billion milestone obscures a significant unresolved question: nobody has agreed on who regulates tokenized equities. Are they securities? Are they derivatives? Are they novel instruments requiring new frameworks? The SEC's position is that most tokenized equity products are securities and require registration. The CFTC has signaled that some structures may fall under its derivatives jurisdiction. The EU's MiCA regulation — the most comprehensive crypto framework currently live — does not directly address tokenized equities. Issuers are operating in the gaps.

This regulatory ambiguity creates a ceiling on institutional adoption. A pension fund or endowment that would otherwise consider tokenized equity exposure cannot currently do so without legal uncertainty about the product's classification. Until the US reaches a legislative settlement on tokenized securities — either through the Digital Asset Market Structure bill currently moving through committee or through SEC guidance — the market will be primarily populated by offshore issuers and crypto-native buyers. The $1.6B milestone is impressive for this constituency. It is a rounding error for traditional equities markets.
